# ClaudePad marketing video: first-draft design

## Goal

Create a compact product video that makes ClaudePad understandable in one
viewing. The draft must show controller mappings, live controller testing,
background control, and PS-button dictation through a configured Vordi or Wispr
Flow shortcut.

## Format

- 1080 × 1920, 30 fps
- 1920 × 1080 landscape adaptation, 30 fps
- Approximately 30 seconds
- Caption-led and understandable without sound
- Tactile sound design, no external copyrighted music

The landscape cut is a native wide composition rather than a crop. It preserves
the 31-second narration and scene timing while rebuilding each scene around
split-screen product proof, wider app panels, and an asymmetric end card.

## Story

1. Open on a physical controller and the PS button: `VOICE IN.`
2. Reveal four useful mappings: Cross sends, Circle stops, Square starts a new
   chat, and PS starts dictation.
3. Show the safe Tester with live buttons, sticks, and shaped values.
4. Prove that controller input continues while ClaudePad stays in the
   background.
5. Show PS-button voice input with Vordi or Wispr Flow, followed by Cross to
   send: `CONTROLLER OUT.`
6. End on the one-command install and the ClaudePad mark.

## Art direction

The visual register is "PlayStation boot sequence meets Claude clay": deep
aubergine, warm terracotta, paper white, and a small cyan signal accent.
Typography is compact and tactile. Motion cuts on button presses, with quick
exponential settles rather than elastic springs.

The design uses product imagery as proof and reconstructed product UI for
legibility. It avoids generic card grids, gradient text, glass effects, and
decorative animation without narrative purpose.

## Accuracy guardrails

- Dictation is shortcut-driven, not a direct Vordi or Wispr Flow API
  integration.
- The first draft describes PS dictation as a configured shortcut and does not
  claim a shipped Vordi preset.
- The install and platform copy is `macOS • MIT open source`.
- The video does not make unverified Windows, Linux, controller, or official
  Claude-shortcut compatibility claims.

## Voiceover mix

The supplied ElevenLabs Maya narration is phrase-edited rather than uniformly
sped up. Long generated pauses are replaced with short intentional gaps, and
each sentence is placed against its matching product scene. Body phrases run
between 1.06× and 1.13× speed; the closing tagline remains slower for emphasis.

Each narration phrase is normalized near -16 LUFS. The ambient bed ramps down
to 30% beneath speech and returns between phrases. The Cross instruction lands
on the send animation, and the closing line begins with the end-card reveal.
